Revised Rules of the Game

Overview
A game of 601, a longer variation of the classic ’01 darts games, is played by starting at 601 points and taking turns throwing three darts. Each turn’s score is subtracted from the running total. Without double-in or double-out rules, players can begin on any scoring segment and finish on any number that brings the score to exactly 0.
The Core Rules
No Double In: A player does not need to hit a double to begin scoring. The very first dart subtracts its exact value from 601.

No Double Out: A player does not need to hit a double to end the game. Any standard segment, including a single, double, or treble, that brings the score to exactly 0 wins the leg.
The Bust Rule: If a player’s three darts add up to a number that takes the score below 0, or leaves the score exactly at 1, the player is busted. The turn immediately ends, and the score is reset to what it was before the turn started.

How to Score Properly Per Leg
Starting: Players throw one dart at the bullseye to determine who throws first. The player closest to the center throws first in the first leg.

Basic Gameplay: Turns: Players take turns throwing up to three darts per round. The total value of the three darts thrown is calculated and subtracted from the current score. Straight In: Scoring begins immediately with the very first dart. A player does not need to hit a double or a specific number to open the score.
Turn Calculations: A player throws three darts per turn. The combined total of these three darts is subtracted from the current score.

Retrieving Darts: Points are only official once the darts are removed from the board and the score is recorded on a scorecard or darts scoring app.

Leg Winner: The first player to reach exactly 0 wins the leg.
Switching: Players alternate who throws first in each subsequent leg.

Understanding the Board Values

To track points accurately per throw, keep the dartboard’s multiplier rings in mind.
•	Single Ring: Wide sections that score the face value of the number.
•	Double Ring: Thin outer ring that doubles the number.
•	Treble Ring: Thin inner ring that triples the number.
•	Outer Bullseye: Worth 25 points.
•	Inner Bullseye: Worth 50 points.
